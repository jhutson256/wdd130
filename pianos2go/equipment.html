What Equipment Should You Use?

General Equipment

This equipment will be used when moving any kind of piano. First, you will need at least an 18” by 30” 4 wheeled furniture dolly. A dolly this size is plenty big enough and strong enough to handle the weight of any piano. If you plan on moving a piano by yourself, then you need to get a fixed wheeled 4-wheel furniture dolly. Next, I recommend you have at least 3 72” by 80” moving blankets, if you're moving a grand piano, then you will need at least 5 of those moving blankets. The last thing that you will need is at least 2 ratchet straps for upright pianos, and at least 4 ratchet straps for grand pianos. This is all of the equipment that you will need to move an upright piano, or a digital piano, you will need extra equipment for a grand piano.

Grand Piano Equipment

On top of the equipment needed in the general equipment section, you will also need a grand board, and a rubber band. First, the grand board. The grand board (shown in the picture next to this section) is what the piano will be laying on and strapped to in order to safely move the piano. There are grand boards for each general size of a grand piano, for example, there are 5', 6', 7', 8', and 9' grand boards. If you have a piano that is in between two of those sizes, for example, 5' 6" or 6' 7", you will want to get the board that is the next size up. Always remember, you'd rather have your board be too long, rather than too short. The next piece of equipment you will need is a rubber band. This will be used to keep the lid from opening when you tip the piano up on its side on the grand board. The rubber band should be at least 3/4" wide and 36" diameter.

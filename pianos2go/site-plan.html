Who is this Website for? Have you ever looked at a piano and thought, "How in the world am I supposed to move that thing without throwing out my back?" This website was made by a professional piano mover to help answer that question. Whether you're planning on moving a piano yourself or you're just curious, this advice can have practical use for you. This website is full of advice on the safest, easiest ways to move any piano. From the best equipment to use, to the best ways to manipulate the weight of a piano making it easier to move, and even the proper ways to safely transport pianos. With this advice, you'll be a piano moving pro just like me, and better yet, you won't have to visit the chiropractor afterwards. Should You Move a Piano? The biggest deciding factor in whether you can move a piano is your overall strength. If you can lift around 500 pounds, then you can move a piano. With that being said, there are still a lot of things you can learn from this website that you can apply to moving anything, so stick around! There are a lot of pianos that you can move by yourself with the right equipment. However, I always recommend having at least one other person to help you move a piano. If you cannot lift around 500 pounds, if you don't have access to a box truck, or if you have to move a piano up or down a flight of stairs, then I recommend hiring professional piano movers. Professional piano movers have extra equipment, training, and insurance to handle those kinds of moves.

Equipment

General Equipment: This equipment will be used when moving any kind of piano. First, you will need at least an 18" by 30" 4 wheeled furniture dolly. A dolly this size is plenty big enough and strong enough to handle the weight of any piano. If you plan on moving a piano by yourself, then you need to get a fixed wheeled 4-wheel furniture dolly. Next, I recommend you have at least 3 72” by 80” moving blankets, if you're moving a grand piano, then you will need at least 5 of those moving blankets. The last thing that you will need is at least 2 ratchet straps for upright pianos, and at least 4 ratchet straps for grand pianos. This is all of the equipment that you will need to move an upright piano, or a digital piano, you will need extra equipment for a grand piano. Grand Piano Equipment: On top of the equipment needed in the general equipment section, you will also need a grand board, and a rubber band. First, the grand board. The grand board (shown in the picture next to this section) is what the piano will be laying on and strapped to in order to safely move the piano. There are grand boards for each general size of a grand piano, for example, there are 5', 6', 7', 8', and 9' grand boards. If you have a piano that is in between two of those sizes, for example, 5' 6" or 6' 7", you will want to get the board that is the next size up. Always remember, you'd rather have your board be too long, rather than too short. The next piece of equipment you will need is a rubber band. This will be used to keep the lid from opening when you tip the piano up on its side on the grand board. The rubber band should be at least 3/4" wide and 36" diameter.

Moving

Upright Pianos: To move an upright piano, you and another person should stand on either side of the piano. First, grab the handle at the back of the piano with one hand, and grab the area in front of the keys with the other hand. Then, count down so both of you lift the piano up at the same time and make sure to lift with your legs. You'll then place the piano on your dolly, so make sure to put the dolly either in front of or behind the piano before you lift. Once the piano is on the dolly, make sure that the back of the piano is flush with the dolly, you can slide it and readjust it while it's on the dolly. You also want the dolly to be centered under the piano. Once the piano is on the dolly you can move the piano! Make sure to have one person on either side of the piano while you are moving the piano and that you both have a good grip on the piano. Once the piano is in the truck, you want to have the back of the piano against the wall of the truck. Place a folded moving blanket on either side of the piano and place one in the middle. Then, strap it in with 2 ratchet straps lined up on top of each other, and have them strapped against the top front panel with the fall board closed. Make sure the straps are nice and tight, and it should be ready to transport! Grand Pianos: First, tuck a blanket under the fall board and close the fall board with the blanket tucked in, then put the excess blanket under the lid and close the lid with the blanket underneath. Next, one person should lift the front corner of the flat side of the piano while someone places a folded blanket underneath the pedals. Please note, the front leg on the flat side of the piano should be off the ground. Then, take off that leg and line your board up with the flat side of the piano. You want to make sure that your dolly is underneath your grand board and placed correctly. With the front of the piano lined up with the front of the grand board, the front of the dolly should be lined up with where the front leg would be. Now, lay an unfolded blanket on the board, this will be used to cover the lid of the piano once it's on its side. Lay the piano down on to the board, the bottom of the piano should only be an inch or two from the edge of the board on the side away from the lid. Get on the other side of the piano where the leg is now raised up and wrap your rubber band around the piano there, so the lid doesn't open. Now, lift the piano with your legs, while somebody is on the other side of the board holding the dolly wheels. Once the piano is on its side, make sure the lid is flush with the board and cover the piano with the blanket you placed down earlier. Once the piano is covered put a folded blanket on the curve of the piano and another folded blanket where the keys are. Then, place two straps on the areas that you just covered with blankets in an x shape. Once that is done, it's ready to move! Always move the piano with one person on either side and always push from the keys. Once the piano is in the truck, make sure that the lid is against the wall of the truck. One strap goes along the top and another strap goes along the bottom. Make sure to tighten them well, and the piano should be ready to transport!
